How it works

Four steps.
One winner.

No deck. No cards. No skill curve. Just nerve. The interface tells you how hot it is — but the exact second the timer fires stays a secret.

01

Open a room

Anyone can spin one up from inside Telegram. Pick the entry fee in credits, share the link, friends pay in to join.

02

Pay the entry

Pay once with TON, get the credits in your wallet. Every entry stacks into one pot. Locked when the game starts.

03

Tap to stay alive

Each tap resets a hidden timer. A meter shows the tension — calm, warming, hot, critical — but never the exact countdown.

04

Last tap wins

When the timer fires, whoever tapped most recently wins 85% of the pot. Credits hit the wallet. Cash out to TON anytime.

Questions.

What is a credit? +
A credit is the in-game unit. 1 TON = 100 credits. The rate is locked, so the pot reads as a clean integer instead of a decimal that swings with the TON price. You can cash out credits to TON from your wallet at any time.
Why hide the timer? +
If everyone could see the seconds tick down, the game would collapse into a single coordinated tap at zero. The hidden timer is the whole point — you have to read the tension and decide when to commit.
Where does the rest of the pot go? +
Winner takes 85%. House (the bot) keeps 10% to pay for servers and TON gas. The person who invited the room creator gets 5%. Splits are paid out the moment the timer fires.
Is there a tap cooldown? +
Yes, one second. Long enough to break a script, short enough to feel responsive. The button shows a thin ring filling back up between taps so you can feel it.
Can I cash out anytime? +
Yes — credits to TON, sent to your wallet. There's a small network fee and a minimum withdrawal of 100 credits.
Where can I play? +
Anywhere there's Telegram. Open the bot 1:1 to play in a private room, or invite the bot into any group chat to play with everyone there. Currently in 12 418 groups across 41 countries.
